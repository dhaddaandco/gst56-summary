GST Council's 56th Meeting — September 3rd, 2025

Overview & Objectives

The 56th Goods and Services Tax (GST) Council meeting, chaired by the Union Finance & Corporate Affairs Minister, introduced a set of next‑generation GST reforms aimed at:

  • Simplifying the tax framework
  • Providing relief to citizens
  • Facilitating trade and commerce
  • Operationalising the Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (GSTAT)

Reforms include rate rationalisation, process improvements, and enhanced refund mechanisms.

Effective Dates

  • September 22, 2025: Most changes in GST rates on services and goods become effective (excluding specific tobacco products).
  • Later notification: For cigarettes, chewing tobacco (e.g., zarda), unmanufactured tobacco and beedi — existing rates continue until compensation‑cess–related loan and interest obligations are discharged.
  • November 1, 2025: Facilitation measures start (provisional refunds, simplified registration, etc.).

Key Changes in GST Rates — Services

Transportation Services

  • Air transport (other than economy class): 12% with ITC → 18% with ITC.
  • Motor vehicles (fuel cost included): 12% with ITC → 18% with ITC. Option: charge 5% with no ITC (if opted).
  • GTA (Goods Transport Agency): Option to charge 5% with no ITC or 18% with full ITC.
  • Containers by Rail: Option to charge 5% with no ITC or 18% with full ITC.
  • Natural Gas/Petroleum through pipeline: Option to charge 5% without ITC or 18% with full ITC.

Trade Facilitation

  • Provisional refunds & streamlined processes
  • Simplified registration and compliance
  • Operational improvements for GSTAT
